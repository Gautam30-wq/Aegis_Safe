THE IMPACT

Ignoring These Issues Costs Everyone

When women are denied equal rights, the consequences ripple across families, economies, and entire nations. Gender inequality costs the global economy an estimated $12 trillion annually in lost GDP.

Countries that invest in women's education and health see higher economic growth, lower child mortality, and stronger democracies. Empowering women is not just a moral imperative; it is an economic and social necessity.

HISTORY & PROGRESS

A Century of Fighting for Equality

The road to gender equality has been long and winding, marked by courageous women and pivotal moments that reshaped history.

1893

First Women's Suffrage

New Zealand becomes the first self-governing country to grant all women the right to vote, sparking a global movement.

1920

19th Amendment (USA)

The United States ratifies the 19th Amendment, prohibiting the denial of the right to vote based on sex.

1945

UN Charter on Equality

The United Nations Charter affirms faith in fundamental human rights and the equal rights of men and women.

1979

CEDAW Adopted

The Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women, often called the "international bill of rights for women," is adopted by the UN.

1995

Beijing Declaration

The Fourth World Conference on Women produces the most progressive blueprint for advancing women's rights.

2015

SDG Goal 5

Gender Equality is established as one of the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals, making it a global priority.

2017

#MeToo Movement

A global reckoning against sexual harassment and assault empowers millions of women to share their stories and demand accountability.

Today

The Fight Continues

Women worldwide continue to push for equal pay, reproductive rights, and freedom from violence. Progress is real, but the work is far from over.

Challenges Today

The Battles That Remain

Despite progress, significant challenges persist worldwide.
Here are the most pressing issues women face today.

📉

Wage Gap

Women globally earn only 77% of what men earn for the same work. At the current rate, it will take an estimated 136 years to close the gap.

🎓

Education Gap

Two-thirds of the world's 773 million illiterate adults are women. In many regions, girls are pulled from school for early marriage.

🛡️

Domestic Violence

Nearly 1 in 3 women have experienced physical or sexual violence by an intimate partner. Many lack access to legal protection.

💼

Workplace Discrimination

Women hold only 29% of senior management roles globally. Glass ceilings and bias in hiring persist across industries.

💓

Healthcare Access

Over 200 million women in developing countries lack access to modern contraception. Maternal mortality remains high in low-income regions.
